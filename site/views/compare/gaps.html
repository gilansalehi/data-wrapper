What you give up

Every framework on the table above earns its weight somewhere. Here is what the industry frameworks have that data-wrapper does not — stated plainly, because pretending otherwise would cost you a rewrite later.

No server rendering, no hydration

Every wrapper fetches its view after the page loads. Content inside a wrapper is invisible to first paint and to crawlers that don't execute JavaScript. If your dynamic content is SEO-critical or your users are on slow connections where first-contentful-paint is the metric, Next, Nuxt, SvelteKit, and Angular solved this years ago and data-wrapper has not.

Coarse-grained invalidation

A flush re-reads every subscribed export of every live component and diffs with Object.is. There is no dependency tracking to narrow the work. That's a feature at page scale — the model fits in your head, and there's no reactive graph to debug — but Solid, Svelte, and Vue update exactly the bindings that depend on what changed. Streaming data, large keyed grids, or animation-rate state will hit the difference.

No router

Pages are pages. *src composes views into layouts, and you can bind it to state to swap views, but there is no URL-driven routing, no history integration, no route guards, no code-splitting story. Every SPA framework in the table has one, often officially maintained.

Templates are invisible to TypeScript

Bindings are strings in HTML attributes. No compiler checks that $text="todoCont" should have been todoCount — at runtime it renders the literal text and warns in the console, which is honest but late. Angular, Svelte, and Vue type-check templates at build time; JSX frameworks get it for free. If your team leans on the compiler to refactor safely, this is the gap that will bite hardest.

Ecosystem of one

No component library, no devtools extension, no Stack Overflow tag, no hiring pool, no LTS policy, and a bus factor of one. React's real product is not the virtual DOM — it's the ten thousand packages, the tutorials, and the person you can hire tomorrow who already knows it. Nothing at this scale can compete with that, and this page won't pretend to.

Young edges

Pre-1.0 means the contract still moves. Some current boundaries, from the limitations section of the guide: bindings don't run inside SVG subtrees; *if and *list bodies need a single root element; keyless list rows fall back to structural identity; views load same-origin only; there is no lifecycle hook matrix beyond the factory and its cleanup. These are documented edges, not hidden bugs — but a mature framework has fewer of them.
